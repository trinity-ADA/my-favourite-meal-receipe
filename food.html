Yam Porridge

My favourite meal is called Yam Porridge. The origin of this dish can be dated as far back as the native kitchens of our African mothers. Yam was a stable food in the early days, pretty much anything carbohydrate was common. However, yam was mostly eaten with fresh palm oil in separate plates but as the art of cooking evolved, yam became more delicious when boiled with fresh palm oil, fresh fish, onions, and other ingredients below.

Let me now take you on a journey into the preparation of my favourite yam porridge.

Ingredients

Serving for three persons

Ingredients Quantity
Yam tuber One
Fresh fish One
Smoked fish Two
Dry fish One
Pepper seed Five
Onion bulb Two
Ugu leaf One big head
Crayfish One derrica
Spices Add to taste

Preparation

  1. Peel the skin off your yam.
  2. Cut it into small chunks and put in the cooking pot.
  3. Add water to cover the yam surface, and place over the fire.
  4. Wash and clean out the fresh fish, smoked fsh snd dry fish.
  5. Soak the dry fish in hot water, while you boil the fresh fish separately.
  6. After about 15 minutes, add your spices, smoked fish, ground pepper, crayfish, sliced onions, and palm oil to the yam.
  7. Leave to cook for 10 more minutes, check for softness of your yam and stir.
  8. Do not allow the water to dry up in the yam. This makes the porridge.
  9. Serve hot
